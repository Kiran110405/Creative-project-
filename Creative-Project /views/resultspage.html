⚠️ Continued high levels of digital engagement may lead to increased dependency on online platforms, where constant interaction becomes a default behaviour rather than a conscious choice. Over time, this can contribute to a reduced attention span, making it harder to focus on tasks without digital interruption. Frequent exposure to notifications, content streams, and rapid interactions can also blur the boundary between online and offline identity, where your sense of self becomes increasingly shaped by digital feedback, algorithms, and external validation. This pattern may result in a decreased awareness of how your data is being collected, interpreted, and used, while reinforcing habits that prioritise immediate engagement over intentional use. As a result, your digital presence becomes more reactive than reflective, driven by stimuli rather than conscious decisions.
